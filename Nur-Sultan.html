City of the future and the capital of Kazakhstan
Nur-Sultan(Astana)

More recently, Nur-Sultan was called Astana. Before that, the city had changed several other names: Akmola, Tselinograd, Akmolinsk.
The city became the capital on December 10, 1997. The capital was moved from the south to the north of the country,
to relieve the traffic and overpopulation of the former capital, Alma-Ata. Besides then still Akmola city possessed an extensive urban territory. The geographical location of the place for the capital was favorable: the economic centers of Kazakhstan became closer, the geographical potential was observed, a favorable landscape and environment prevailed around, and the transport infrastructure was already developed.
Nur-Sultan population is about 1,228,800 (2021).
